  • The "Where On Mars?" Project

    Visualising the ExoMars rover candidates landing sites

    The "Where On Mars?" project started as an ESAC Trainee Project in collaboration with CartoDB. The objective was to prototype a web map visualization of the ESA's ExoMars 2018 rover candidate landing sites, with the general purpose of increasing awareness of the mission to the general public.
    It aims at growing as an open source and collaborative project. Learn more

  • The ExoMars 2018 Mission

    Searching for Life on Mars

    The 2018 mission of the ESA's ExoMars programme will deliver a European rover and a Russian surface platform to the surface of Mars. The ExoMars rover will travel across the Martian surface to search for signs of life. It will collect samples with a drill and analyse them with next-generation instruments. ExoMars will be the first mission to combine the capability to move across the surface and to study Mars at depth. Learn more.

  • The Landing Site Selection Process

    How to find a suitable landing site for the rover?

    During October 2013, the European Space Agency (ESA) and the Russian Federal Space Agency (Roscosmos) appointed a Landing Site Selection Working Group (LSSWG) to make recommendations to the agencies regarding landing sites for the ExoMars 2018 mission. After a call to the scientific community for landing sites, four candidate landing sites were shortlisted for further investigation, both in term of scientific interest and engineering safety. The next step should select two final candidates during the fall 2015. Learn more
